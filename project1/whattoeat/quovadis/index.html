A bit of History

Quo Vadis was originally established in the early 20th century, in 1926, by an Italian named Peppino Leoni. It was initially a Italian restaurant and became known for its authentic Italian cuisine and welcoming atmosphere.
Over the years, Quo Vadis attracted a diverse clientele, including artists, writers, musicians, and celebrities. It became a popular haunt for the Soho artistic and literary community.
The restaurant changed ownership several times over the years, with different proprietors maintaining its reputation for good food and a convivial atmosphere.
Quo Vadis solidified its position as a culinary landmark in London's Soho district, known for its charm and classic British and European cuisine.
In 2008, renowned chef Jeremy Lee took over the kitchen at Quo Vadis. Under his leadership, the restaurant gained renewed acclaim for its seasonal and inventive menu, showcasing the best of British and European flavors.
The restaurant's interior was also refurbished and revitalized, retaining its historic charm while infusing it with a contemporary feel. Quo Vadis includes a members' club, making it a hub for social and cultural activities in Soho. The club features exclusive events, gatherings, and a private space for members.
