History of The Tower Of London

The tower was created in the early 1066 shortly after the Norman Conquest of England in 1066 by William the Conqueror. Its construction began as part of the effort to secure and control the city of London. Over the centuries, the Tower served various roles, including as a royal palace, a prison for high-profile captives, and a treasury. It was the site of important historical events, such as the execution of Anne Boleyn and the "Princes in the Tower." One of the Tower's most famous functions is safeguarding the Crown Jewels of the United Kingdom. These magnificent symbols of monarchy, including crowns, scepters, and orbs, have been stored and displayed here for centuries. The Tower also played a military role, serving as a formidable fortress and a base for English forces during conflicts. Its iconic White Tower, built by William the Conqueror, remains a symbol of this period. In the modern age, the Tower of London transitioned into a popular historical and cultural attraction. Today, it welcomes millions of visitors each year who come to explore its rich history and the legends surrounding it. The Tower of London was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988, recognizing its historical and architectural significance. Visiting the Tower of London provides a captivating journey through the annals of British history, offering a glimpse into medieval life, the intrigues of royal courts, and the fortitude of a structure that has endured for nearly a millennium.

Why Visit The Tower Of London?

The Tower of London has a history spanning over a thousand years. It has served as a royal palace, a prison, and a treasury. Exploring the Tower let you to go back in time and discover its past, including its role in key events in British history. The Tower is home to the Crown Jewels, a importance collection of royal family, including crowns, scepters, and orbs. Visitors have the opportunity to witness these magnificent symbols of monarchy up close, but you can't touch it. You get to meet the The Yeoman Warders, also known as Beefeaters, serve as guides and historians. Their guided tours provide fascinating insights into the Tower's history and legends. The Tower Bridge, adjacent to the Tower, offers beautifyl views of the River Thames and London's skyline. A visit to the Tower can be also give you with a walk along the Tower Bridge walkways. The Tower features interactive exhibits that make history come alive, given visitor a visual of how it was back then. From medieval armor displays to tales of espionage, there's something to engage visitors of all ages.
